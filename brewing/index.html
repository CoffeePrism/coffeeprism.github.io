The Art & Science of Coffee Brewing

Brewing great coffee is both an art and a science. It requires understanding the variables that affect extraction and how they interact to create the perfect cup.

Our brewing guides are designed to help you master various brewing methods, whether you're a beginner or an experienced coffee enthusiast looking to refine your technique.

Each guide includes step-by-step instructions, recommended equipment, and tips for troubleshooting common issues.

Key Brewing Factors

  • Grind Size: Affects extraction rate and flavor balance
  • Water Temperature: Influences solubility and extraction efficiency
  • Brew Ratio: Determines strength and concentration
  • Brew Time: Controls total extraction and flavor development
  • Water Quality: Impacts clarity and flavor purity

Essential Brewing Tips

Use Freshly Roasted Beans

Coffee is at its best 7-21 days after roasting. Look for beans with a roast date and avoid those without one.

Grind Just Before Brewing

Coffee begins to lose flavor immediately after grinding. For the best results, grind only what you need right before brewing.

Water Quality Matters

Coffee is 98% water. Use filtered water with the right mineral content for optimal extraction and flavor.

Measure Your Coffee

Use a scale to ensure consistency. A general starting ratio is 1:16 (coffee to water) for most brewing methods.

Mind Your Temperature

For most brewing methods, water temperature should be between 195-205°F (90-96°C) for optimal extraction.

Keep Equipment Clean

Residual coffee oils can turn rancid and affect flavor. Clean your equipment thoroughly after each use.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Coffee Tastes Too Bitter

Bitterness often indicates over-extraction. Try:

  • Using a coarser grind
  • Reducing brew time
  • Lowering water temperature slightly
  • Checking if your coffee is too darkly roasted for your preference

Coffee Tastes Too Sour or Weak

Sourness typically indicates under-extraction. Try:

  • Using a finer grind
  • Increasing brew time
  • Raising water temperature
  • Ensuring proper coffee-to-water ratio

Inconsistent Results

Inconsistency can be frustrating. Try:

  • Using a scale to measure coffee and water precisely
  • Timing your brew process
  • Using a consistent grind size (consider upgrading your grinder)
  • Following a systematic process each time

Coffee Tastes Flat or Stale

Flat or stale flavors can come from several sources:

  • Check coffee freshness (beans should be used within 2-4 weeks of roast date)
  • Store beans properly in an airtight container away from light, heat, and moisture
  • Grind just before brewing
  • Clean your equipment thoroughly to remove old coffee oils
